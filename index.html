Story: ASK EVERYTHING BUT WRITE DOWN THE POSITIVES! FOCUS ON WHAT IS RELEVANT TO THE SURGICAL CONSULTATION
  • Onset: Sequence of events and why presenting today; preceding events
  • Where coming from and what did they do there?
  • Interventions, medications, studies, consultations
  • If they have tubes, drains or devices: are they functioning? What have the outputs been?
  • Palliating, provoking, Quality, radiation, severity
  • Prior episodes, evolution over time
  • Trauma Details: Time of injury, Mechanism (MVC/MCC speed, location of force transfer), Seatbelt/helmet/airbags/protective devices, Post-injury course, Identified injuries, Therapies/interventions
  • Constitutional: fever, chills, night sweats, weight loss, anorexia (last MEAL), travel, sick contacts, diet changes
  • Neurologic: headache, dizziness, numbness, tingling, weakness, presyncope/syncope, tremors, convulsions/seizures, unsteady gait
  • Skin: jaundice, rash, lesions, petechiae, itching
  • ENMT: vision changes, hearing changes, ear or eye pain, sore throat, congestion, runny nose, hoarseness, dysphagia
  • Respiratory: shortness of breath, cough, wheezing
  • Cardiovascular: chest pain, palpitations
  • Gastrointestinal: nausea, vomiting, last BM and gas, change in bowel habits, abdominal pain
  • Genitourinary: dysuria, urine retention (last void), hematuria, discharge
  • Musculoskeletal: pain, joint swelling, stiffness, myalgias, trauma
  • Psychiatric: anxiety, depression, restlessness, suicidal ideation
  • Heme/Lymph: bleeding tendency, bruising tendency, petechiae, swollen nodes
  • Menstrual: LMP, menopausal
  • Previous diagnoses
  • Chronic conditions
  • Previous surgeries
  • Home medications
  • Current hospital medications
  • Allergies (cross-check)
  • Emergency contact #
  • Smoking history
  • Alcohol use
  • Recreational/illicit drugs
  • Caffeine, Sexual history, Nutrition/diet, Occupation, Religion/spiritual beliefs
  • Major hereditary diseases (cardiovascular, diabetes, cancer, bleeding disorders)
  • Liver/biliary/pancreatic disease
  • Other significant familial conditions
  • Gen: Well appearing in no acute distress. Alert and oriented x3
  • Neuro: strength and sensation intact BUE+BLE, CN II–XII grossly intact, EOMI
  • Resp: Non-labored breathing. No use of accessory muscles, no retractions
  • CV: Hemodynamically stable
  • Pulse: palpable b/l DP/PT pulses, radial/ulnar pulses
  • Abd: soft, non-tender, non-distended; no voluntary or involuntary guarding; no rebound or jolt tenderness
  • Wound: Incisions clean, dry, intact
  • Head: Normocephalic, atraumatic, no deformities, abrasions, tenderness, oropharynx clear, no blood in nares or ears, hearing intact
  • Neuro: GCS 15, Pupils equal and reactive to light bilaterally, extraocular muscles intact, gross motor and sensory intact in all 4 extremities
  • Face: Atraumatic, no deformities, abrasions, tenderness
  • Chest: clear to auscultation bilaterally, non-tender, atraumatic, pulling on incentive spirometer
  • CVS: RRR, hemodynamically stable
  • Abdomen: soft, non-tender, non-distended, no voluntary or involuntary guarding, no jolt or rebound tenderness
  • Pelvis: Stable
  • Upper Extremities: Atraumatic, no deformities, abrasions, tenderness, Bilateral radial pulses palpable, brisk capillary refill
  • Lower Extremities: Atraumatic, no deformities, abrasions, tenderness, brisk capillary refill, Bilateral femoral, DP, PT pulses palpable
  • Spine: No obvious cervical, lumbar, or thoracic spine deformities, no step offs, no tenderness, trachea midline
  • Perineum: No gross blood in the perineum, rectal exam deferred, anal wink reflex intact
  • GCS: Eye opening (E4 spontaneously, E3 to voice, E2 to pain, E1 none), Verbal (V5 oriented, V4 confused, V3 inappropriate words, V2 incomprehensible, V1 none), Motor (M6 obeys, M5 localizes, M4 withdraws, M3 decorticate, M2 decerebrate, M1 none)
